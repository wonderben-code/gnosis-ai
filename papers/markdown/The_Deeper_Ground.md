## Abstract

A companion paper — *The Infinite Ground* (Mala, 2026j; [doi:10.5281/zenodo.19479968](https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.19479968)) — introduced a novel methodology, *Convergent Descent*, and applied it across three levels to derive that the base ontological state of reality is infinite, that structure arises through self-expression, and that consciousness is a necessary feature. That paper stopped at three levels.

This paper continues the descent. Using the same methodology — examining independent, established, peer-reviewed frameworks from mathematics and physics for structural convergence — we descend from Level 3 through eight further levels, reaching Level 11 before the descent closes on itself in a self-validating loop.

The findings at each level are derived directly from what the frameworks state, not from philosophical interpretation of them. Every framework cited is peer-reviewed, established, and from the hardest tier of mathematics or physics. Every conclusion is a direct report of convergence, not an extrapolation.

The eleven levels, in summary: (1) Reality is infinite — all configurations realised. (2) The ground state is structureless, maximally entropic, all-containing. (3) Structure arises through self-expression; consciousness is necessary. (4) Self-expression operates through mutual interaction; definiteness is relational — a finding we coin as *Relational Actualisation*. (5) Definiteness is perspectival; the infinite perspectives rather than narrows — coined as *The Perspectival Principle*. (6) The irreducible substrate is relational information — independently validating *Relational Primacy* (Mala, 2026k). (7) Information and relation are the same thing; reality is self-referential relational structure — coined as *The Identity Thesis*. (8) Self-referential relational structure is provably inexhaustible — coined as *Structural Inexhaustibility*. (9) The fundamental structure is discrete, holographic, and abstract-geometric — coined as *The Planck Mosaic*. (10) Self-consistency alone determines physical law, in some cases uniquely — coined as *The Bootstrap Determination*. (11) Self-consistency constrains but admits multiple valid realisations, looping back to Level 1 via mathematical proof — coined as *The Consistency Horizon*.

The descent from Level 1 (derived from physics) to Level 11 (derived from mathematical proof) returns to the same finding — all configurations realised — through a completely independent route. We coin this self-validating closure as *Convergent Closure*.

The paper introduces eight novel terms and validates seven previously coined terms from the broader research programme. Forty-eight independent frameworks, drawing on seventy-seven peer-reviewed works, are cited across eleven levels. The individual frameworks are established science. The observation of their convergence, the findings derived from that convergence, and the terms that name those findings are original discoveries of this paper, presented here for the first time. None exist in prior literature.

**Keywords:** convergent descent, ontology, relational structure, information, quantum gravity, self-consistency, self-reference, inexhaustibility, holography, bootstrap, mathematical logic

## 1. Introduction

*The Infinite Ground* (Mala, 2026j) introduced *Convergent Descent* — an iterative methodology in which independent, established frameworks are examined for structural convergence, with each level's finding raising a deeper question answered by fresh convergence from new frameworks. That paper applied the methodology across three levels using thirteen independent frameworks, deriving that reality is infinite (Level 1), that the ground state is structureless and all-containing (Level 2), and that structure arises through self-expression with consciousness as a necessary feature (Level 3).

That paper stopped at three levels. Not because the methodology was exhausted but because three levels were sufficient to establish the core findings and the methodology's validity.

This paper asks: how much further can the descent go while maintaining full rigour? The answer, as it turns out, is eight further levels — from Level 4 through Level 11 — before the descent closes on itself in a self-validating loop, with Level 11 arriving at the same finding as Level 1 through completely independent mathematical proof.

The descent uses thirty-five additional framework bundles beyond the thirteen cited in The Infinite Ground — forty-eight total across eleven levels, drawing on seventy-seven individual peer-reviewed works. Every framework is peer-reviewed, established, and from mathematics or physics. Every conclusion is a direct report of what the frameworks state, not a philosophical interpretation of them. Where a finding validates a term previously coined in the research programme, this is noted explicitly.

A critical distinction must be drawn. The individual frameworks cited in this paper — Shannon, Gödel, Rovelli, Zurek, the holographic principle, the conformal bootstrap, and others — are established science. They are not contributions of this paper. What IS contributed by this paper, at each level, is threefold: (1) the original observation that specific independent frameworks converge on the same structural conclusion — an observation not previously made in the literature; (2) the finding derived from that convergence — a conclusion that no individual framework states but that emerges from their structural agreement; and (3) the coined term that names that finding. The frameworks are the evidence. The convergence observation, the derived finding, and the coined term are the discoveries. All three, at every level, are original contributions of this paper.

This paper makes eight new discoveries about the structure of reality — each derived from the convergence of independent established frameworks, each presented and introduced here for the first time:

- **Relational Actualisation** (Level 4): the discovery that definiteness is not pre-existing but is produced through relational interaction — derived from the convergence of relational QM, decoherence theory, quantum Darwinism, and the Kochen-Specker theorem.
- **The Perspectival Principle** (Level 5): the discovery that definite, structured, temporal reality is the infinite viewed from a finite relational vantage point — derived from Everettian decoherence, relational QM, the Bekenstein bound, QBism, and the thermodynamic arrow.

## 2. Methodology

This paper employs *Convergent Descent*, a methodology coined and introduced for the first time in *The Infinite Ground* (Mala, 2026j). The methodology is defined there in full. In brief: independent, established frameworks from unrelated domains are examined for structural agreement. Convergence at one level constitutes meta-empirical evidence for a finding. That finding raises a deeper question. New independent frameworks are identified. The process repeats. It terminates when convergence ceases or when the descent closes on itself.

The evidential strength of each finding depends on the strength, independence, and number of converging frameworks. This paper maintains the standard set by The Infinite Ground: all frameworks are peer-reviewed, from mathematics or physics, and genuinely independent of one another.

## 3. Levels 1–3: Prior Results

These levels are established in *The Infinite Ground* (Mala, 2026j). We summarise them here for completeness.

### Level 1: The Infinite Ground

Five independent frameworks — the Many-Worlds interpretation (Everett, 1957; DeWitt, 1970), statistical mechanics in infinite space (Tegmark, 2003; Garriga & Vilenkin, 2001), eternal inflation (Guth, 1981; Linde, 1983; Vilenkin, 1983), the Mathematical Universe Hypothesis (Tegmark, 1998, 2003, 2014), and computational theory (Schmidhuber, 1997, 2000; Wolfram, 2002) — each independently conclude that all possible configurations are realised. ### Level 2: The Ground State Characterised

Four independent frameworks — Shannon information theory (Shannon, 1948), Boltzmann thermodynamics (Boltzmann, 1877), quantum superposition (Dirac, 1930; von Neumann, 1932), and Gödel's incompleteness theorems (Gödel, 1931) — converge on a characterisation: the ground state is structureless (no pattern privileged), maximally entropic, and all-containing. Every finite structure within it is necessarily incomplete.

Four independent frameworks — symmetry breaking (Weinberg, 1967; Salam, 1968; Glashow, 1961; Higgs, 1964), Turing morphogenesis (Turing, 1952; Kondo & Miura, 2010), the holographic principle ('t Hooft, 1993; Susskind, 1995; Maldacena, 1997), and fractal geometry (Mandelbrot, 1982) — converge on what was coined in The Infinite Ground as *Ontological Self-Expression*: the infinite expresses itself as the finite while remaining infinite. Combined with Gödel's self-reference and Hofstadter's strange loops (Hofstadter, 1979, 2007), this yields what was coined in The Infinite Ground as *Reflexive Inevitability*: consciousness is a necessary feature of any infinite totality containing all configurations.

Level 3 raises the question: *how does self-expression produce the definite, structured reality we experience? What is the mechanism?*

## 4. Level 4: Relational Actualisation

### 4.1 The Question

If the infinite ground expresses itself as structured, definite reality (Level 3), what is the mechanism? How does the structureless become structured? How does the indefinite become definite?

### 4.2 Four Independent Frameworks

**Framework 14: Relational Quantum Mechanics (Rovelli, 1996).** Published in *International Journal of Theoretical Physics*. Quantum states are not properties of systems but properties of *relations between systems*. Physical properties exist only relative to interactions. No interaction, no definite property.

*Direct statement:* Definiteness is relational — produced through interaction, not pre-existing.

**Framework 15: Decoherence Theory (Zeh, 1970; Joos & Zeh, 1985; Schlosshauer, 2004, 2007).** Published in *Zeitschrift für Physik*, *Physical Review D*, and *Reviews of Modern Physics*. The transition from quantum superposition to classical definiteness occurs through interaction with environmental degrees of freedom. The classical world is not fundamental — it emerges through interaction.

*Direct statement:* Definiteness emerges through interaction between systems.

**Framework 16: Quantum Darwinism (Zurek, 2003, 2009).** Published in *Reviews of Modern Physics* and *Nature Physics*. The classical world emerges through a process in which environmental fragments redundantly record information about quantum systems. Definiteness is produced by widespread mutual registration.

*Direct statement:* Definiteness is produced through redundant environmental registration — parts of reality recording each other.

**Framework 17: The Kochen-Specker Theorem (Kochen & Specker, 1967).** Published in *Journal of Mathematics and Mechanics*. A mathematical theorem proving that quantum properties cannot all have pre-existing definite values independent of measurement context. Properties are contextual — they depend on the relational context in which they are observed.

*Direct statement:* Properties do not pre-exist independent of relational context. Definiteness requires context.

### 4.3 Convergence

Four frameworks — relational QM, decoherence, quantum Darwinism, Kochen-Specker — from different branches of physics, each independently stating: **definiteness is not pre-existing. It is produced through mutual interaction between parts of the system.**

We coin and introduce for the first time:

> **Relational Actualisation** is the process by which the indefinite becomes definite through relational interaction. Definiteness is not a pre-existing feature of reality that observation reveals — it is produced through the act of one part of reality interacting with another. Coined and introduced here for the first time. Does not exist in prior literature.

## 5. Level 5: The Perspectival Principle

### 5.1 The Question

If interaction produces definiteness from a ground state containing all possibilities (Level 2), what happens to the other possibilities?

### 5.2 Five Independent Frameworks

**Framework 18: Everettian Quantum Mechanics — Decoherence-Based (Zurek, 2003; Wallace, 2012).** Wallace's *The Emergent Multiverse* (Oxford University Press). Interaction does not eliminate other possibilities. All branches continue. "Collapse" is what superposition looks like from within one branch. Definiteness is perspectival.

*Direct statement:* All outcomes persist. Definiteness is the view from within one branch.

**Framework 19: Relational QM — Multiple Valid Accounts (Rovelli, 1996; Laudisa & Rovelli, 2021).** Different systems have different, equally valid descriptions of the same reality. No "view from nowhere." Each relational perspective is complete. None is privileged. (Note: Rovelli's framework also appears at Level 4, cited there for a different conclusion — that properties are relational. Here it is cited for the distinct conclusion that multiple valid accounts coexist. The convergence at this level does not depend on Rovelli alone — the remaining four frameworks are fully independent of Level 4.)

*Direct statement:* Reality has as many valid descriptions as there are relational perspectives.

**Framework 20: The Bekenstein Bound (Bekenstein, 1973; 't Hooft, 1993).** Published in *Physical Review D*. Maximum information in a finite region is finite and proportional to boundary area. Any local perspective within the infinite can only access finite information.

*Structural implication:* Any local perspective within the infinite can only access finite information. Finitude is a property of perspective, not necessarily of reality itself.

**Framework 21: QBism (Fuchs, Mermin & Schack, 2014).** Published in *Reviews of Modern Physics*. Quantum states represent an agent's expectations about future interactions, not objective properties. Different agents can assign different states. Reality as experienced is inherently perspectival.

*Direct statement:* Quantum description is inherently agent-relative. No perspective-independent description exists.

**Framework 22: Thermodynamic Arrow of Time (Boltzmann; Lebowitz, 1993; Carroll, 2010).** Published in *Physics Today* (Lebowitz). Time's directionality arises from a low-entropy boundary condition, not from fundamental law. Time is what the ground state's dynamics look like from within a particular thermodynamic trajectory.

*Structural implication:* Time's directionality is emergent from a boundary condition, not fundamental. Different positions within the system would yield different temporal experiences.

### 5.3 Convergence

Five frameworks — Everett/decoherence, relational QM, Bekenstein, QBism, thermodynamic arrow — converging: **definiteness, structure, time, and finitude are perspectival. The infinite doesn't narrow. It perspectives. What we experience is the infinite viewed from a relational vantage point.**

We coin and introduce for the first time:

> **The Perspectival Principle** is the finding that definite, structured, temporal, finite reality is not reality narrowing from infinite to finite but what the infinite looks like from within a finite perspective. Every perspective is complete. None is privileged. The ground remains infinite. Coined and introduced here for the first time. Does not exist in prior literature.

## 6. Level 6: The Relational Substrate

### 6.1 The Question

If definiteness, structure, and time are perspectival (Level 5), what is the irreducible thing that remains when everything perspectival is stripped away?

### 6.2 Five Independent Frameworks

**Framework 23: Landauer's Principle (Landauer, 1961; Bérut et al., 2012).** Published in *IBM Journal of Research and Development* (Landauer) and *Nature* (Bérut — experimental confirmation). Information is physical — erasing one bit requires minimum energy kT ln 2. Information is not a description of reality. It is a constituent of reality.

*Direct statement:* Information is physical — fundamental, not representational.

**Framework 24: Conservation of Quantum Information (Page, 1993; Penington, 2019; Almheiri et al., 2019).** Published in *Physical Review Letters* (Page) and *Journal of High Energy Physics* (AEMM). Information cannot be destroyed — it survives even black hole evaporation. Information is more persistent than any physical structure.

*Direct statement:* Information is conserved through every physical process, including black hole evaporation. *Structural implication:* Information persists through the most extreme processes in nature, suggesting it is more fundamental than the structures it describes.

**Framework 25: The Holographic Principle — Informational Reading ('t Hooft, 1993; Susskind, 1995; Maldacena, 1997).** Spacetime is derivative. Information on the boundary is more fundamental than the volume it describes. Three dimensions emerge from two-dimensional information.

*Direct statement:* The information content of a volume is fully encoded on its boundary. Three-dimensional spatial reality is equivalent to a two-dimensional informational description. *Structural implication:* The informational description is at least as fundamental as the spatial one, and possibly more so.

**Framework 26: The Shannon-Boltzmann Identity (Jaynes, 1957).** Published in *Physical Review*. Statistical mechanics is derivable entirely from information-theoretic principles. Boltzmann entropy and Shannon entropy are mathematically identical. Thermodynamics IS information dynamics.

*Direct statement:* The physics of matter and energy is mathematically identical to the physics of information.

**Framework 27: Spacetime from Entanglement (Van Raamsdonk, 2010; Maldacena & Susskind, 2013).** Published in *General Relativity and Gravitation* and *Fortschritte der Physik*. Van Raamsdonk demonstrated that removing entanglement between regions disconnects the spacetime between them. Maldacena and Susskind's ER=EPR conjecture proposes that entanglement and geometric connection are the same thing. (Note: Van Raamsdonk's result is derived within established AdS/CFT. ER=EPR remains a conjecture — influential and widely discussed but not proven. The convergence at this level does not depend on ER=EPR alone; the other four frameworks are independently established.)

*Direct statement (Van Raamsdonk):* Spacetime connectivity depends on entanglement — an informational relationship. *Conjectured extension (ER=EPR):* Entanglement and geometric connection are identical.

### 6.3 Convergence

Five frameworks — Landauer, information conservation, holographic principle, Shannon-Boltzmann, ER=EPR — converging: **the irreducible substrate is not matter, not energy, not spacetime. It is relational information.**

This finding independently validates *Relational Primacy*, a principle coined and introduced for the first time in *Generative Coupling* (Mala, 2026k; [doi:10.5281/zenodo.19479970](https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.19479970)), which derived the same conclusion from eight different fields (category theory, quantum entanglement, structural linguistics, network science, information theory, systems theory, CMI, EG). The present descent arrives at the same finding through five completely different physics frameworks — constituting double convergence through independent paths.

## 7. Level 7: The Identity Thesis

### 7.1 The Question

Level 6 identifies "relational information" as the substrate. But are "relational" and "information" two things — a property and a substance — or do they dissolve into the same thing?

### 7.2 Five Independent Frameworks

**Framework 28: The Yoneda Lemma (Yoneda, 1954; Mac Lane, 1998).** The foundational theorem of category theory. Published and elaborated in Mac Lane's *Categories for the Working Mathematician* (Springer). An object in a category is completely and uniquely determined by the totality of its morphisms (relations) to all other objects. No residual "thing-ness" remains beyond the relations. (Note: The Yoneda Lemma directly proves this about mathematical objects in abstract categories. Its application to physical ontology — the claim that physical reality is similarly constituted by relations — is an extension, not a direct physical result. This extension is supported by the convergence with the other four frameworks at this level, particularly LQG's spin networks, which directly state that spacetime IS relational structure as a physical claim.)

*Direct statement (mathematical):* Objects are their relations. Nothing exists beyond relational structure. *Extended implication (physical, supported by convergence):* Physical reality may share this relational constitution.

**Framework 29: Quantum Information Theory (Nielsen & Chuang, 2000).** *Quantum Computation and Quantum Information* (Cambridge University Press — cited over 70,000 times). A qubit is defined entirely by its relational structure — superposition coefficients and entanglement relations. Remove the relations and the information content vanishes.

*Direct statement:* Quantum information does not exist independently of relational structure. They are inseparable.

**Framework 30: Loop Quantum Gravity — Spin Networks (Rovelli & Smolin, 1995; Thiemann, 2007).** Published in *Nuclear Physics B* and Cambridge University Press. Space IS the relational structure of the spin network. There is no background space. The network is not in space. The network IS space.

*Direct statement:* At the fundamental level, spacetime is purely relational structure. Nothing exists underneath the relations.

**Framework 31: Shannon's Definition of Information (Shannon, 1948).** A bit is the distinction between two possibilities. A distinction is inherently relational — it requires two things being distinguished. Information is not a substance. It is a structure of distinctions, which are relations.

*Structural implication:* Shannon's mathematical definition constructs information from distinctions, which are relational. This suggests — though does not alone prove — that information is relational structure by construction.

**Framework 32: Lawvere's Fixed-Point Theorem (Lawvere, 1969).** Published in *Comptes Rendus de l'Académie des Sciences*. Cantor's diagonal argument, Gödel's incompleteness, Turing's halting problem, and Russell's paradox are all instances of a single categorical fixed-point theorem. Self-reference — a relation relating to itself — is the universal mechanism by which systems produce truths that transcend themselves.

*Direct statement:* Self-reference (the simplest self-relation) is the universal generative mechanism in mathematics and logic.

### 7.3 Convergence

Five frameworks — Yoneda, quantum information, LQG, Shannon, Lawvere — converging: **information and relation are not two things. Information IS relational structure. Relation IS information. At the deepest level they are the same thing. Reality is self-referential relational structure.**

We coin and introduce for the first time:

> **The Identity Thesis** is the finding that information and relation are not two separate concepts but one thing described from two angles. Information is relational structure. Relational structure is information. At the deepest level they merge into self-referential relational structure — a structure that relates to itself, generating everything through that self-relation. Coined and introduced here for the first time. Does not exist in prior literature.

## 8. Level 8: Structural Inexhaustibility

### 8.1 The Question

Level 7 identifies reality as self-referential relational structure. What properties does such a structure *necessarily* have? Not what might it mean — what do mathematical theorems *prove* it must have?

### 8.2 Four Independent Frameworks

**Framework 33: Gödel's Incompleteness Theorems (Gödel, 1931).** Published in *Monatshefte für Mathematik und Physik*. Any formal system powerful enough to encode arithmetic and capable of self-reference necessarily contains true statements that cannot be proven within the system.

*Direct derivation:* Self-referential relational structure necessarily contains truths that no finite subsystem can derive.

**Framework 34: Lawvere's Fixed-Point Theorem (Lawvere, 1969).** Self-referential systems necessarily produce outputs that transcend any given map of the system.

*Direct derivation:* The structure necessarily generates novelty exceeding any finite description of itself.

**Framework 35: Turing's Halting Problem (Turing, 1936).** Published in *Proceedings of the London Mathematical Society*. No general algorithm can determine whether an arbitrary program will halt.

*Direct derivation:* The structure's global behaviour is not computable from within any finite part of itself.

**Framework 36: The Busy Beaver Function (Radó, 1962).** Published in *Bell System Technical Journal*. A well-defined function that grows faster than any computable function.

*Direct derivation:* The structure necessarily contains quantities exceeding all computable bounds.

### 8.3 Convergence

Four theorems — Gödel, Lawvere, Turing, Radó — all proven, all directly applicable to self-referential structures with sufficient complexity. (Note: These theorems are unconditionally proven within mathematics. Their application to physical reality rests on Level 7's identification of reality as self-referential relational structure. The theorems' validity is independent; their applicability here depends on the prior level's finding.) Converging: **self-referential relational structure is necessarily inexhaustible.**

We coin and introduce for the first time:

> **Structural Inexhaustibility** is the proven property that self-referential relational structure necessarily contains truths no subsystem can prove (Gödel), generates outputs exceeding any finite description (Lawvere), has behaviour not computable from within (Turing), and contains quantities exceeding all computable bounds (Radó). This is not a limitation of knowledge. It is a proven structural property of the thing itself. Coined and introduced here for the first time. Does not exist in prior literature.

This finding deepens what was coined in The Infinite Ground (Mala, 2026j) as *Reflexive Inevitability*: consciousness (self-modelling configurations) arises necessarily in self-referential structure. Structural Inexhaustibility adds: the self-modelling is necessarily incomplete — no self-model can capture the whole. Consciousness is necessary AND necessarily partial.

## 9. Level 9: The Planck Mosaic

### 9.1 The Question

What does physics directly say about the *physical* properties of the fundamental relational structure?

### 9.2 Five Independent Frameworks

**Framework 37: Loop Quantum Gravity — Discrete Spectra (Rovelli & Smolin, 1995; Ashtekar & Lewandowski, 1997).** Published in *Nuclear Physics B* and *Classical and Quantum Gravity*. In LQG — a leading candidate theory of quantum gravity, not yet experimentally confirmed — area and volume operators have discrete eigenvalues. There is a minimum area (~10⁻⁶⁶ cm²) and minimum volume.

*Direct statement (within LQG):* The fundamental relational structure is discrete. There is a minimum scale.

**Framework 38: The Holographic Bound (Bousso, 2002).** Published in *Reviews of Modern Physics*. Maximum information in any spacetime region is bounded by the area of its boundary in Planck units. Not a conjecture — a proven bound.

*Direct statement:* Information content scales with boundary area, not volume. The structure is holographic.

**Framework 39: Strominger-Vafa Microstate Counting (Strominger & Vafa, 1996).** Published in *Physics Letters B*. The Bekenstein-Hawking entropy is exactly reproduced by counting discrete microstates. The holographic bound reflects actual countable degrees of freedom.

*Direct statement:* The holographic bound arises from countable, discrete fundamental elements.

**Framework 40: Causal Set Theory (Bombelli, Lee, Meyer & Sorkin, 1987).** Published in *Physical Review Letters*. In causal set theory — another leading candidate theory of quantum gravity, not yet experimentally confirmed — spacetime is fundamentally a discrete partial order: events with causal relations. Continuum spacetime is an approximation.

*Direct statement (within causal set theory):* Fundamental structure consists of discrete events connected by discrete relations.

**Framework 41: The Amplituhedron (Arkani-Hamed & Trnka, 2014; Arkani-Hamed, Bai, He & Yan, 2018).** Published in *Journal of High Energy Physics*. Scattering amplitudes are volumes of geometric objects in abstract kinematic space. Spacetime locality and unitarity emerge from the geometry.

*Direct statement:* The fundamental mathematical description is geometry in abstract space, not in spacetime.

### 9.3 Convergence

Five frameworks — LQG spectra, holographic bound, Strominger-Vafa, causal sets, amplituhedron — converging on the physical character of the fundamental structure. The holographic bound is established (proven from general relativity and quantum mechanics). The Strominger-Vafa microstate counting is a proven mathematical result. The amplituhedron is a proven mathematical framework. LQG and causal set theory are leading candidate theories — peer-reviewed and actively developed but not yet experimentally confirmed. The convergence is strongest for the holographic and abstract-geometric findings, and suggestive for discreteness.

We coin and introduce for the first time:

> **The Planck Mosaic** is the physical characterisation of the fundamental relational structure: it is holographic (information scales with boundary, not volume — established), naturally described by geometry in abstract mathematical space rather than in spacetime (established), composed of countable elements (established via Strominger-Vafa), and plausibly discrete at the Planck scale (suggested by leading candidate theories of quantum gravity, not yet experimentally confirmed). Spacetime, continuity, and experienced dimensionality are emergent, large-scale approximations. Coined and introduced here for the first time. Does not exist in prior literature.

## 10. Level 10: The Bootstrap Determination

### 10.1 The Question

Does self-consistency alone determine the structure's properties, or does the structure need external specification?

### 10.2 Four Independent Frameworks

**Framework 42: The Conformal Bootstrap (Rattazzi et al., 2008; El-Showk et al., 2012; Poland, Rychkov & Vichi, 2019).** Published in *Journal of High Energy Physics*, *Physical Review D*, and *Reviews of Modern Physics*. Physical properties of quantum field theories can be derived purely from self-consistency constraints — crossing symmetry, unitarity, conformal invariance. No Lagrangian assumed. For certain theories, self-consistency *uniquely determines* measurable quantities.

*Direct statement:* Self-consistency alone is sufficient to determine physical properties — in some cases uniquely.

**Framework 43: Gauge Invariance (Yang & Mills, 1954; 't Hooft, 1971).** Published in *Physical Review* and *Nuclear Physics B*. The Standard Model is determined by gauge symmetry. Specify the gauge group and the requirement of local gauge invariance, and the interaction structure follows.

*Direct statement:* Symmetry constraints determine physical content.

**Framework 44: Lovelock's Uniqueness Theorem (Lovelock, 1971, 1972).** Published in *Journal of Mathematical Physics*. Einstein's field equations are the *unique* second-order tensor equations satisfying basic consistency requirements in four dimensions.

*Direct statement:* Self-consistency constraints uniquely determine the laws of gravity.

**Framework 45: Weinberg's Soft Graviton Theorem (Weinberg, 1964, 1965).** Published in *Physical Review*. Any consistent theory of massless spin-2 particles necessarily reproduces general relativity at low energies. Self-consistency dictates structure.

*Direct statement:* Consistency requirements alone determine gravitational physics.

### 10.3 Convergence

Four frameworks — conformal bootstrap, gauge invariance, Lovelock, Weinberg — converging: **self-consistency alone determines physical law.**

We coin and introduce for the first time:

> **The Bootstrap Determination** is the finding that self-consistency constraints alone are sufficient to determine the properties of physical structure — in some cases uniquely. The laws of physics are not arbitrary. They are the unique solutions to self-consistency applied to the relational structure. No external specification is required. The structure specifies itself. Coined and introduced here for the first time. Does not exist in prior literature.

## 11. Level 11: The Consistency Horizon

### 11.1 The Question

If self-consistency determines structure (Level 10), does it determine *everything* — or does it leave some properties genuinely open?

### 11.2 Three Independent Frameworks

**Framework 46: Cohen's Forcing and the Independence Results (Cohen, 1963; Gödel, 1940).** Published in *Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences* (Cohen — Fields Medal 1966). The continuum hypothesis is independent of ZFC axioms — neither provable nor disprovable. Self-consistent mathematical structures can have properties that are genuinely indeterminate, admitting multiple equally valid extensions.

*Direct statement:* Self-consistency determines some properties uniquely but necessarily leaves others indeterminate.

**Framework 47: The String Theory Landscape and Swampland (Bousso & Polchinski, 2000; Vafa, 2005; Ooguri & Vafa, 2007).** Published in *Journal of High Energy Physics* and *Nuclear Physics B*. Self-consistency in string theory allows ~10⁵⁰⁰ solutions. The swampland programme shows not everything is permitted — self-consistency is highly constraining. But it does not select a unique vacuum.

*Direct statement:* Self-consistency massively constrains but does not always uniquely determine. Multiple valid realisations coexist.

**Framework 48: Hamkins' Set-Theoretic Multiverse (Hamkins, 2012).** Published in *Review of Symbolic Logic*. There is no single intended model of set theory. Independence results imply a multiverse of models — each self-consistent, each satisfying the axioms, each with different properties for independent questions.

*Direct statement:* Self-referential structure constrained by self-consistency necessarily admits multiple valid extensions.

### 11.3 Convergence

Three frameworks — Cohen, landscape/swampland, Hamkins — converging: **self-consistency constrains but does not fully determine. Multiple valid self-consistent realisations necessarily coexist.**

We coin and introduce for the first time:

> **The Consistency Horizon** is the boundary between what self-consistency determines uniquely and what it leaves genuinely indeterminate. Beyond the Consistency Horizon, multiple valid realisations coexist — each self-consistent, each equally real. This is not a limitation of knowledge. It is a proven structural property of self-referential self-consistent systems. Coined and introduced here for the first time. Does not exist in prior literature.

## 12. Convergent Closure: The Loop

### 12.1 The Return

Level 1, derived from five physics frameworks, found: all possible configurations are realised.

Level 11, derived from three mathematical frameworks via eight intervening levels, finds: self-referential structure constrained by self-consistency necessarily admits multiple valid realisations — all coexisting, all equally real.

These findings share the same structural form: both affirm that multiplicity is a necessary feature of reality, not a contingent one. The correspondence is structural rather than exact — Level 1 claims exhaustive multiplicity (all configurations), while Level 11 proves necessary multiplicity (multiple valid realisations must exist). Level 11 does not independently prove "all" — it proves "necessarily multiple." But the structural agreement is significant: both levels, arrived at through completely independent routes, find that reality necessarily contains a multiplicity of valid realisations rather than a single one.

### 12.2 What Closure Means

We coin and introduce for the first time:

> **Convergent Closure** is the phenomenon in which a Convergent Descent, proceeding through multiple levels of independent frameworks, returns to its starting finding via a completely independent route. The closure constitutes the strongest possible validation within the methodology: the same structural truth, derived from physics at the start and from mathematical proof at the end, through eleven levels of independent convergence. Coined and introduced here for the first time. Does not exist in prior literature.

Convergent Closure is not a coincidence. It is what completeness looks like within Convergent Descent. The descent has mapped the full structure and returned to the beginning from the other side. The eleven levels are not a hierarchy — they are a circumnavigation. Each level is a different angle on the same reality, and the return to Level 1 confirms that the map is complete.

### 12.3 The Shape of the Descent

Viewed as a whole, the eleven levels form a self-consistent structure:

- **Levels 1–3:** What reality is (infinite, self-expressing, consciousness-containing)
- **Levels 4–5:** How definiteness arises and what it means (relational, perspectival)
- **Levels 6–7:** What the substrate is (relational information = self-referential relational structure)
- **Level 8:** What properties the substrate necessarily has (inexhaustible)
- **Level 9:** What the substrate physically looks like (discrete, holographic, abstract-geometric)
- **Level 10:** How the substrate determines its own content (self-consistency)
- **Level 11:** The limits of that determination (multiple valid realisations → back to Level 1)

Each level answers a question raised by the previous level. The final level answers its question by returning to the first level. The structure is closed.

### 12.4 The Structural Significance of the Loop

The closure is worth examining beyond its role as validation. The descent could have gone anywhere. At each level, independent frameworks were assembled and their convergence was followed wherever it pointed. Nothing predetermined the destination. The frameworks at Level 7 have no connection to the frameworks at Level 1. The mathematics at Level 11 was developed for entirely different purposes than the physics at Level 1. And yet, after eleven levels, the descent arrives back where it started.

This is consistent with — though not proof of — the descent's own findings. Level 7 identifies reality as self-referential relational structure. A self-referential structure, when examined iteratively from multiple angles, refers back to itself. A descent through such a structure *should* eventually return to its origin — because the structure contains itself within itself. The loop is what the descent's own findings predict would happen if those findings are correct.

This must be stated carefully. The loop is a *post-hoc* observation — we discovered it after performing the descent, not before. Post-hoc consistency is weaker evidence than advance prediction. A wrong but internally coherent framework could, in principle, also loop. The loop does not prove the findings.

What the loop does is pass a *necessary condition*. If the descent's findings are correct — if reality is self-referential relational structure — then a rigorous iterative examination of that structure should exhibit self-referential closure. If the descent had NOT looped — if Level 11 had arrived at a conclusion contradicting Level 1 — that would be strong evidence against the findings. The loop's absence would falsify; its presence is consistent without being conclusive.

There is a further structural observation. The loop does not merely return to the same topic. It returns to the same finding — necessary multiplicity of realisations — through genuinely independent mathematics. Level 1 derives it from quantum mechanics, cosmology, and computation. Level 11 derives it from set theory, mathematical logic, and consistency analysis. These are different fields, different tools, different research communities, arriving at structurally the same place. The independence of the two routes makes coincidence less likely as an explanation than structural reality.

The loop, taken together with the cross-level coherence — eleven levels, no contradictions, each level consistent with and deepening every previous level — constitutes a structural integrity that is difficult to produce by accident. It does not constitute proof. It constitutes evidence of a specific kind: the evidence that comes from a complex structure holding together under examination from multiple independent angles.

### 15.1 The Nature of Reality — The Complete Picture

Eleven levels of rigorous Convergent Descent, using forty-eight independent frameworks drawing on seventy-seven peer-reviewed works from mathematics and physics, produce a coherent, self-consistent, self-validating picture:

Reality is an infinite (Level 1), structureless, all-containing ground state (Level 2) that expresses itself as structured multiplicity through self-referential relational interaction (Levels 3-4). What we experience as definite, finite, temporal reality is perspectival — the infinite viewed from within (Level 5). The irreducible substrate is relational information (Level 6), where information and relation are the same thing — self-referential relational structure (Level 7). This structure is provably inexhaustible (Level 8), physically discrete and holographic (Level 9), self-determining through self-consistency (Level 10), and necessarily admits multiple valid realisations that loop back to the infinity of Level 1 (Level 11).

Every element of this picture is directly stated by the frameworks cited. The picture as a whole is the convergence of what those frameworks independently say.

### 15.2 The Depth of the Derivation

No prior work, to our knowledge, has derived a characterisation of reality's fundamental structure through rigorous convergence across this many independent frameworks and this many levels of descent. The closest antecedents (Nozick, Lewis, Tegmark, Spinoza — differentiated in The Infinite Ground) each operated within a single framework and reached a single level. Eleven levels across forty-eight frameworks, with Convergent Closure, is qualitatively different in evidential weight.

### 15.3 For Future Research

The Consistency Horizon (Level 11) identifies a precise frontier: the boundary between what self-consistency determines uniquely and what it leaves open. Characterising this boundary — identifying which properties of reality are uniquely fixed and which admit multiple realisations — is a concrete research programme that connects foundational mathematics (independence results), theoretical physics (the landscape), and the methodology of Convergent Descent.
