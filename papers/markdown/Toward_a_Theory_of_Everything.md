## Abstract

Companion papers in this research programme derived, through convergence of established results across physics and mathematics, the Self-Referential Relational Principle (SRRP): reality is self-referential relational structure, with contextuality, emergence, informational primacy, self-referential limits, and perspectival irreversibility as its characteristic expressions.

This paper asks: if the SRRP is correct, what constraints does it impose on a theory of everything (ToE)? What must a successful ToE look like? Where do the major research programmes in theoretical physics naturally align with those constraints? And where do those programmes already overlap in ways the SRRP predicts?

We derive eight structural constraints from the SRRP and examine seven major programmes — string theory / M-theory, loop quantum gravity, causal set theory, the amplituhedron programme, asymptotic safety, topos-theoretic approaches, and the conformal bootstrap — identifying where each programme's existing strengths naturally align with specific constraints. We identify areas of convergence between programmes, propose a synthesis direction, and catalogue the specific unsolved mathematical problems that separate current knowledge from a complete theory.

This paper does NOT propose a theory of everything. It provides a structurally grounded roadmap toward one — specifying what a successful theory must look like, where existing programmes naturally align, and what remains to be solved.

**A note on humility.** The SRRP constraints are derived from the research programme's own findings. The alignment observations regarding specific physics programmes are made with general structural knowledge and the author welcomes specialist engagement and review.

**Keywords:** theory of everything, quantum gravity, SRRP, self-referential relational structure, string theory, loop quantum gravity, conformal bootstrap, amplituhedron, structural constraints

## 1. Introduction

The search for a theory of everything — a single mathematical framework that unifies all fundamental forces and reproduces all known physics — has been the central aspiration of theoretical physics for decades. String theory, loop quantum gravity, causal set theory, and other programmes each approach this problem from different starting points with different mathematical tools.

Companion papers in this research programme addressed a different question: not "what is the specific theory?" but "what is the structural nature of the thing the theory describes?" Through convergence of established results across quantum foundations, quantum gravity, mathematical logic, information theory, and thermodynamics, the programme derived the Self-Referential Relational Principle (SRRP): reality is self-referential relational structure (*The Convergence Map*, Mala, 2026o; doi:10.5281/zenodo.19520611). The companion paper *Mathematical Foundations* (Mala, 2026n; doi:10.5281/zenodo.19520692) proved that reflexive mathematical structures with this property necessarily exhibit five specific features.

This paper bridges the structural characterisation and the search for a specific theory. If the SRRP is correct — if reality is self-referential relational structure — then any successful theory of everything must be compatible with this characterisation. The SRRP imposes constraints. These constraints can be used to identify alignments with existing programmes, observe convergences between them, and specify what a successful synthesis must achieve.

The paper does not propose a theory of everything. The gap between structural constraints and a specific theory is vast — it requires constructing new mathematics, solving open problems, and making testable predictions. What this paper provides is a roadmap: the structural shape of the destination, derived from convergence evidence, against which current efforts can be measured.

## 2. Eight Structural Constraints from the SRRP

The SRRP and its five characteristic expressions, combined with findings from the broader research programme, impose eight constraints on any candidate theory of everything. These are structural requirements — any theory that violates them is inconsistent with the SRRP characterisation.

### Constraint 1: Relational Foundation

*From SRRP Expression (i) — Contextuality.*

The theory must be formulated in terms of relations, not intrinsically propertied objects. Fundamental entities in the theory must be defined by their relational structure — their connections to other entities — not by standalone intrinsic properties. A theory built on elementary particles with intrinsic properties existing independently of all relations violates this constraint.

### Constraint 2: Background Independence

*From SRRP Expression (ii) — Emergence.*

Spacetime must emerge from the theory, not be assumed by it. The theory cannot presuppose a fixed spacetime manifold as a background arena in which physics happens. Spacetime geometry, dimensionality, and topology must be derived, not inputs. Any theory formulated on a fixed background spacetime violates this constraint unless it can be reformulated background-independently.

### Constraint 3: Holographic Structure

*From SRRP Expression (iii) — Informational Primacy, and The Planck Mosaic (Mala, 2026l).*

The theory must exhibit holographic scaling — the degrees of freedom of a region must scale with its boundary area, not its volume. Boundary descriptions must be complete — a boundary theory must contain all the information of the bulk. This is a constraint on the theory's information-theoretic structure, not merely on its solutions.

### Constraint 4: Informational Primitives

*From SRRP Expression (iii) — Informational Primacy.*

Information must be a primitive concept in the theory, not derived from more fundamental material or geometric concepts. The theory should be expressible in informational terms, with matter, energy, and spacetime emerging from informational structure rather than the reverse.

### Constraint 5: Self-Consistency as Determinant

*From The Bootstrap Determination (Mala, 2026l).*

The theory's content should be determined by self-consistency constraints rather than by freely chosen parameters. Physical constants, particle content, and force structure should follow from the requirement that the theory be self-consistent, not from external inputs. A theory with many free parameters that must be set by hand is less aligned with this constraint than one whose parameters are fixed by consistency.

### Constraint 6: Self-Referential Completeness

*From SRRP Expression (iv) — Self-Referential Limits, and Structural Inexhaustibility (Mala, 2026l).*

The theory must be self-referential — capable of describing its own physical substrate. A theory of everything must include the physical systems doing the theorising within its scope. But by Theorem 1 of *Mathematical Foundations* (Mala, 2026n), any such theory is necessarily incomplete from within — it cannot fully describe itself. A successful ToE must embrace this: it is a theory of everything except its own complete description.

### Constraint 7: Perspectival Structure

*From SRRP Expression (v) — Perspectival Irreversibility, and The Perspectival Principle (Mala, 2026l).*

The theory must produce observer-dependent descriptions from observer-independent foundations. Different perspectives within the theory must yield different but equally valid descriptions. Time's arrow, irreversibility, and the appearance of classical definiteness must emerge perspectivally from time-symmetric, quantum-coherent foundations. The theory must not build irreversibility or classicality in by hand.

### Constraint 8: Multiple Valid Realisations

*From The Consistency Horizon (Mala, 2026l).*

The theory must admit multiple valid realisations — different self-consistent configurations of physical constants, particle content, or vacuum structure. Self-consistency constrains but does not uniquely determine all properties. A theory that produces exactly one solution with no alternatives is in tension with this constraint. The space of solutions should be rich but not unconstrained.

## 3. The Major Programmes

Seven major research programmes in theoretical physics bear on the search for a theory of everything. Each is described here by its core structural idea — the feature most relevant to the SRRP constraints.

**String theory / M-theory.** Fundamental objects are one-dimensional strings; five superstring theories are unified as limits of eleven-dimensional M-theory (Witten, 1995); AdS/CFT (Maldacena, 1997) provides a holographic formulation; the landscape admits ~10⁵⁰⁰ vacua.

**Loop quantum gravity.** Spacetime is quantised directly without assuming a background; fundamental structures are spin networks whose geometry produces discrete area and volume spectra (Rovelli & Smolin, 1995).

**Causal set theory.** Spacetime is fundamentally a discrete partial order — "order + number = geometry" (Bombelli et al., 1987).

**The amplituhedron programme.** Scattering amplitudes are volumes of geometric objects in abstract kinematic space; spacetime, locality, and unitarity emerge from the geometry (Arkani-Hamed & Trnka, 2014).

**Asymptotic safety.** Gravity is non-perturbatively renormalisable via an ultraviolet fixed point (Reuter, 1998); spectral dimension flows to ~2 at the Planck scale.

**Topos-theoretic approaches.** Physics is formulated within topos theory, providing alternative mathematical universes with internal logic and perspectival structure (Isham, Butterfield, Döring, ~2000–present).

**The conformal bootstrap.** Physical theories are derived from self-consistency constraints alone — crossing symmetry, unitarity, conformal invariance — without assuming a Lagrangian (Rattazzi et al., 2008; El-Showk et al., 2012).

## 4. Alignment Between Programmes and Constraints

This section does not evaluate or rank the major programmes. Each programme represents decades of work by specialist communities and any assessment from outside those communities would be presumptuous. Instead, this section notes — positively — where each programme's existing strengths naturally align with specific SRRP constraints.

### 4.1 Natural Alignments

**String theory / M-theory** aligns naturally with Constraint 3 (holographic structure) through AdS/CFT, and with Constraint 8 (multiple valid realisations) through the landscape. The web of dualities connecting apparently different formulations resonates with the SRRP's relational character.

**Loop quantum gravity** aligns naturally with Constraint 1 (relational foundation) and Constraint 2 (background independence) — it was designed with these principles at its core. Its spin network formulation is inherently relational.

**Causal set theory** aligns naturally with Constraint 1 (relational foundation), Constraint 2 (background independence), and Constraint 4 (informational primitives) through its formulation "order + number = geometry."

**The amplituhedron programme** aligns naturally with Constraint 1 (relational — geometry, not objects), Constraint 2 (background independence — spacetime emerges), Constraint 4 (informational — geometric information is primitive), and Constraint 5 (self-consistency — content determined by positivity conditions).

**Asymptotic safety** aligns naturally with Constraint 2 (background independence) and Constraint 5 (self-consistency — the UV fixed point is a self-consistency condition).

**Topos-theoretic approaches** align naturally with Constraint 1 (relational), Constraint 6 (self-referential completeness — topoi can model their own internal logic), Constraint 7 (perspectival structure — different topoi provide different perspectives), and Constraint 8 (multiple valid realisations).

**The conformal bootstrap** aligns naturally with Constraint 1 (relational — defined by correlations) and Constraint 5 (self-consistency as determinant — this is its defining feature).

### 4.2 The Observation

No single programme currently aligns with all eight constraints. Each captures aspects of the structure the SRRP describes. This is not a criticism of any programme — each was developed to address specific problems, not to satisfy externally imposed structural constraints. The observation is simply that the structural characterisation provided by the SRRP spans features that are currently distributed across multiple programmes.

## 5. Convergence Between Programmes

The SRRP predicts that programmes studying the same underlying reality should exhibit structural convergence — shared features that arise not from mutual influence but from the common structure they're investigating. Several such convergences are already visible.

### 5.1 Observed Convergences

**Spacetime emergence.** LQG, causal sets, CDT, the amplituhedron, and string theory (via AdS/CFT) all independently conclude that spacetime is emergent rather than fundamental. This is predicted by SRRP Constraint 2.

**Dimensional reduction.** LQG, CDT, asymptotic safety, and Hořava-Lifshitz gravity all independently find spectral dimension flowing to ~2 at the Planck scale. This convergence across independent programmes with different mathematical foundations is striking and unexplained by any single programme.

**Holographic structure.** String theory (via AdS/CFT), LQG (via Chern-Simons boundary theory), and causal sets (via boundary-volume scaling) all exhibit or approach holographic structure. This is predicted by SRRP Constraint 3.

**Self-consistency as structure.** The conformal bootstrap and the amplituhedron both derive physical content from self-consistency and positivity constraints alone, without assuming a Lagrangian. This convergence on a bootstrap-like methodology is predicted by SRRP Constraint 5.

**Relational foundations.** LQG (spin networks), causal sets (causal partial orders), the amplituhedron (positive geometries), and topos theory all formulate physics in terms of relational or structural primitives rather than intrinsically propertied objects. This is predicted by SRRP Constraint 1.

### 5.2 The SRRP Interpretation

The SRRP predicts these convergences. If reality is self-referential relational structure, then any programme investigating reality's fundamental level will encounter the features of that structure — regardless of its starting point. The convergences between programmes are not coincidences. They are what the SRRP predicts: different approaches to the same self-referential relational reality, each capturing aspects of the same structure from different mathematical angles.

This is the same pattern observed in the convergence programme itself — independent frameworks converging because they're investigating the same thing. The ToE programmes exhibit this pattern because they are further instances of it.

## 6. The Synthesis Direction

### 6.1 Which Ingredients

The alignments identified in Section 4 suggest that a successful synthesis would draw on:

**From the amplituhedron programme:** The principle that physics is geometry in abstract space — that spacetime, locality, and unitarity emerge from positive geometric structure.

**From the conformal bootstrap:** The principle that self-consistency alone determines physical content — deriving physics from consistency constraints without assuming a Lagrangian.

**From loop quantum gravity:** Background independence and discrete relational structure — spin networks as relational quantum geometry.

**From string theory / AdS/CFT:** Holographic duality — the deep connections between geometry, entanglement, and information revealed by the Ryu-Takayanagi formula and quantum error correction.

**From causal set theory:** The informational formulation "order + number = geometry" and discrete causal structure.

**From topos theory:** The mathematical framework for handling multiple valid descriptions, perspectival structure, and self-referential completeness.

### 6.2 The Shape and Its Limits

The SRRP suggests a successful synthesis would be a self-consistent, background-independent theory formulated in terms of relational-informational primitives, whose physical content is determined by positivity and consistency conditions, with holographic structure, admitting multiple valid realisations, from which spacetime, matter, and the Standard Model emerge, with perspectival irreversibility arising from the theory's own structure, and necessarily incomplete from within.

This describes a shape, not a theory. The SRRP constrains. It does not construct. Converting this structural outline into a specific mathematical framework is the work that this paper cannot do — that work requires the specialist tools and expertise that the major programmes have developed over decades. The synthesis direction is a compass heading, not a map.

## 7. The Unsolved Problems

Between the current state of knowledge and a complete theory of everything, specific mathematical problems must be solved. This section names them, without claiming to solve them.

### 7.1 The Background-Independence Problem for String Theory

String theory's perturbative formulation requires a fixed background. A fully background-independent non-perturbative formulation of M-theory does not yet exist. AdS/CFT provides background independence in a specific (AdS) setting but a general formulation is an open problem.

### 7.2 The Matter Problem for Loop Quantum Gravity

LQG quantises geometry but does not naturally incorporate the Standard Model. Deriving the specific particle content and gauge structure of the Standard Model from spin network dynamics is an unsolved problem.

### 7.3 The Dynamics Problem for Causal Sets

Causal set theory defines the kinematic structure (discrete causal order) but the dynamical law — how causal sets grow or which causal sets are realised — is not fully determined. The classical sequential growth models (Rideout-Sorkin) exist but a quantum dynamics is incomplete.

### 7.4 The Realistic Extension of the Amplituhedron

The amplituhedron is currently formulated for N=4 super-Yang-Mills theory — a supersymmetric theory that is not realistic physics. Extending the positive-geometric framework to realistic theories (the Standard Model, non-supersymmetric gauge theories, gravity) is ongoing work.

### 7.5 The Conformal Bootstrap Beyond CFT

The conformal bootstrap applies to conformal field theories. Extending bootstrap methods to non-conformal theories, to gravity, and to cosmology is an active research frontier. The S-matrix bootstrap (bootstrap for scattering amplitudes) is one direction.

### 7.6 The Concrete Predictions Problem for Topos Theory

Topos-theoretic approaches provide a powerful abstract framework but have not yet produced specific physical predictions. Connecting the abstract categorical structure to measurable physics is the programme's central open problem.

### 7.7 The Unification of Mathematical Frameworks

Perhaps the deepest problem: the mathematical languages of the different programmes are largely incommensurable. String theory uses differential geometry and algebraic geometry. LQG uses connection dynamics and spin foams. The amplituhedron uses combinatorial geometry. Topos theory uses categorical logic. Translating between these languages — finding the mathematical framework that contains all of them as special cases — is itself an unsolved problem.

### 7.8 The SRRP-Specific Problem

The reflexive domain structure identified by the SRRP and formalised in *Mathematical Foundations* must be connected to specific physics. Which reflexive object, in which category, with which retraction, produces the Standard Model? This is the specific question the SRRP adds to the list of open problems. It is currently unanswered.

## 8. The SRRP Prediction

### 8.1 The Prediction

The SRRP makes a specific prediction about the search for a theory of everything: **the major programmes should converge.**

If reality is self-referential relational structure, then programmes investigating reality's fundamental level from different mathematical angles are investigating the same structure. They should, over time, discover the same features — because the features are properties of the structure, not artifacts of the approach.

This prediction is already partially borne out. The convergences identified in Section 5 — spacetime emergence, dimensional reduction, holographic structure, bootstrap methodology, relational foundations — are visible. The SRRP predicts more will appear as the programmes develop.

### 8.2 Testability

This makes the SRRP indirectly testable through the ToE research programme:

- **If the programmes converge** — if string theory, LQG, causal sets, the amplituhedron, and the bootstrap increasingly agree on structural features — this is evidence for the SRRP. The convergence is predicted.

- **If the programmes diverge** — if they increasingly disagree on structural features, producing incompatible pictures of fundamental reality — this is evidence against the SRRP. The divergence contradicts the prediction.

- **If a successful ToE is eventually constructed** — its mathematical structure can be checked against the SRRP characterisation. If it is a reflexive, self-referential, relational structure exhibiting all five SRRP expressions, the SRRP is confirmed. If it has a fundamentally different character, the SRRP is refuted.

The SRRP does not predict the specific theory. It predicts the structural shape of the theory. That shape is checkable.

## 9. Novel Contributions

This paper makes five contributions. The SRRP and its derivation are from companion papers. The application to the ToE search is original.

**1. The eight SRRP constraints on a theory of everything.** Derived from the SRRP and the research programme's findings. These structural requirements on any candidate ToE are stated here for the first time.

**2. The alignment mapping.** The identification of natural alignments between each major programme's existing strengths and specific SRRP constraints — presented positively, not as evaluation. The observation that no single programme currently aligns with all eight constraints and that the SRRP's structural characterisation spans features distributed across programmes.

**3. The convergence observation.** The identification that existing convergences between programmes (spacetime emergence, dimensional reduction, holographic structure, bootstrap methodology, relational foundations) are predicted by the SRRP.

**4. The synthesis direction.** The proposal that a successful synthesis would combine positive geometric structure (amplituhedron), bootstrap methodology (conformal bootstrap), relational background independence (LQG), holographic duality (AdS/CFT), informational primitives (causal sets), and topos-theoretic meta-framework — constrained by the SRRP.

**5. The SRRP prediction and testability.** The specific prediction that ToE programmes should converge, and the identification that this makes the SRRP indirectly testable through the research programmes it constrains.

## 10. Conclusion

If the SRRP is correct, a theory of everything must satisfy eight structural constraints derived from it. The major research programmes in theoretical physics each align with some of these constraints but none currently encompasses all eight. The SRRP's structural characterisation spans features that are presently distributed across programmes.

This distribution is itself informative. The programmes already exhibit convergences — spacetime emergence, holographic structure, relational foundations, dimensional reduction, bootstrap methodology — that the SRRP predicts. If the programmes continue to converge, the SRRP's characterisation is supported. If they diverge, it is challenged. The prediction is specific and checkable.

The synthesis direction is visible: a self-consistent, background-independent, holographic theory formulated in relational-informational primitives, determined by positivity and consistency constraints, admitting multiple realisations, producing perspectival irreversibility, and necessarily incomplete from within. The mathematical ingredients exist across current programmes. The unification of those ingredients — and the specific unsolved problems catalogued in Section 7 — is the work that remains.

This paper provides a structurally grounded roadmap. The construction of the theory itself belongs to the mathematical physics community.
