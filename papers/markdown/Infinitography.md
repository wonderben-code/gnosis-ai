## Abstract

Companion papers in this research programme — *The Infinite Ground* (Mala, 2026j; doi:10.5281/zenodo.19479968) and *The Deeper Ground* (Mala, 2026l; doi:10.5281/zenodo.19488612) — derived, through convergence of forty-eight independent frameworks across eleven levels, that reality is an infinite configuration space whose structure is self-referential, relational, holographic, inexhaustible, and self-determining through self-consistency. Those papers answered the question "what is reality?"

This paper asks the next question: "Given that this is what reality is, what does established physics and mathematics say about how systems traverse, optimise, and expand within it?"

Through convergence of twenty-five additional independent frameworks from physics and mathematics, we derive three principles:

1. **The Navigation Principle** — interaction is the steering mechanism of state-space traversal. What a system interacts with, how frequently, and at what rate determines its trajectory. Derived from nine frameworks including the principle of stationary action, Feynman's path integral, unitary evolution, the quantum Zeno/anti-Zeno effects, and the adiabatic theorem.

2. **The Optimisation Principle** — given preferences over configurations, optimal traversal is achievable through sequential locally optimal interactions, with convergence to preferred configurations mathematically guaranteed. Derived from eight frameworks including calculus of variations, Pontryagin's maximum principle, Bellman's optimality, Lyapunov stability, and Prigogine's dissipative structures.

3. **The Expansion Principle** — interaction naturally produces expanding, cumulative, irreversible multi-configuration presence across state space. Simultaneous occupation is the quantum default. Expansion is exponential. Contraction is mathematically forbidden. Derived from eight frameworks including quantum superposition, entanglement growth, unitarity, tensor product spaces, and Liouville's theorem.

These three principles combine into a **Unified Principle**: preference-directed interaction produces an irreversibly expanding, cumulative, stable presence across preferred configurations, with the process being self-reinforcing and irreversible.

We further derive, through five additional frameworks, that these principles apply to conscious agents — not by analogy but because conscious agents are physical systems within the configuration space these principles describe.

From these findings, a field emerges: **Infinitography** — the study, exploration, and navigation of the infinite configuration space of reality. We define six subfields, coin the AI discipline of **Computational Infinitography** for deploying AI systems within the space, and show how previously coined fields (Epistemic Genesis, Collective Machine Intelligence) operate within specific regions of the configuration space.

The paper is descriptive, not prescriptive. It derives the mechanisms. It does not recommend their use. All principles, fields, and terms introduced in this paper are coined here for the first time and do not exist in prior literature.

**Keywords:** configuration space, navigation, optimisation, expansion, state-space traversal, interaction, quantum mechanics, variational principles, infinitography

## 2. The Navigation Principle

### 2.1 The Question

What does established physics and mathematics say about how systems traverse state spaces — what determines which configurations are reached from a given starting configuration, and what determines the trajectory?

### 2.2 Nine Independent Frameworks

**Framework 1: The Principle of Stationary Action (Euler, 1744; Lagrange, 1788; Hamilton, 1834).** The deepest organising principle in physics. Every fundamental theory can be derived from it. A system traverses the path between configurations where the action functional is stationary (first variation equals zero). Among all possible trajectories, the realised one is where the action is stationary. Experimentally confirmed across all domains of physics.

*Direct statement:* Traversal follows specific paths determined by the action. Not random. Structured.

*Caveat:* The principle determines trajectory shape between specified endpoints. It does not determine which endpoints are reached.

**Framework 2: Feynman's Path Integral (Feynman, 1948).** Published in *Reviews of Modern Physics*. A quantum system transitioning between configurations doesn't follow one path — all paths contribute simultaneously, each weighted by e^(iS/ℏ). The observed trajectory emerges from constructive interference of paths near the stationary-action point and destructive interference of distant paths.

*Direct statement:* All paths contribute to transitions. The realised trajectory is the interference pattern of all possible paths, dominated by stationary-action paths.

*Caveat:* The path integral describes transitions between specified configurations. It is a computational framework whose mathematical foundations have known rigour issues in some settings, though its physical results are confirmed.

**Framework 3: Unitary Evolution (Schrödinger, 1926; Dirac, 1930).** Textbook quantum mechanics. The state vector evolves through Hilbert space according to the Hamiltonian. Evolution is unitary — continuous, reversible, and information-preserving.

*Direct statement:* State-space traversal is continuous, reversible, and information-preserving. No information is lost during traversal.

**Framework 4: Decoherence (Zeh, 1970; Joos & Zeh, 1985; Zurek, 2003; Schlosshauer, 2007).** Established, experimentally confirmed physics. Interaction with the environment produces apparent classical definiteness from quantum superposition. Under Everettian interpretation, this constitutes branching — the traversal splits into multiple trajectories.

*Direct statement:* Interaction with the environment determines which basis the system appears definite in — shaping what the traversal "looks like" from within.

*Caveat:* The branching ontology is interpretation-dependent (Everettian). Decoherence itself is established physics.

**Framework 5: Noether's Theorem (Noether, 1918).** Published in *Nachrichten der Gesellschaft der Wissenschaften zu Göttingen*. Every continuous symmetry corresponds to a conserved quantity. Conservation laws partition state space into accessible and inaccessible regions relative to any configuration.

*Direct statement:* Not all of state space is accessible from a given configuration. Conservation laws constrain which regions are reachable.

**Framework 6: Poincaré Recurrence (Poincaré, 1890).** Published in *Acta Mathematica*. For bounded, measure-preserving systems, the system necessarily returns arbitrarily close to any previous configuration given sufficient time. Unitary quantum evolution is measure-preserving.

*Direct statement:* The system necessarily revisits neighbourhoods of previous configurations. Trajectories are recurrent.

*Caveat:* Recurrence times for complex systems can be astronomically long. The theorem guarantees recurrence but says nothing about when.

**Framework 7: Quantum Zeno and Anti-Zeno Effects (Misra & Sudarshan, 1977; Itano et al., 1990; Kofman & Kurizki, 2000).** Theoretically derived and experimentally confirmed. Frequent measurement inhibits state transitions (Zeno). At certain frequencies, measurement accelerates transitions (anti-Zeno).

*Direct statement:* The frequency and nature of interaction directly determines the rate of state-space traversal — either freezing the current state or accelerating transitions.

**Framework 8: The Adiabatic Theorem (Born & Fock, 1928).** Published in *Zeitschrift für Physik*. If the Hamiltonian changes slowly, the system follows its evolving eigenstate smoothly. If rapidly, the system can jump to entirely different eigenstates.

*Direct statement:* The rate of environmental change determines whether traversal is smooth (following the current landscape) or discontinuous (jumping to new regions).

**Framework 9: Symplectic Structure (Hamilton, 1834; Arnold, 1978).** Phase space has a symplectic structure. Hamilton's equations define flows that preserve this structure (Liouville's theorem). The geometry constrains which flows are physically realisable.

*Direct statement:* The geometry of state space constrains which trajectories are permitted. Certain paths are geometrically forbidden.

### 2.3 Convergence

Nine frameworks — stationary action, path integral, unitary evolution, decoherence, Noether, Poincaré, Zeno/anti-Zeno, adiabatic theorem, symplectic structure. All established physics and mathematics.

Four of these independently identify interaction as the determinant of traversal:

- Decoherence: interaction determines the basis of apparent definiteness
- Quantum Zeno: interaction frequency controls transition rate
- Quantum anti-Zeno: interaction frequency can accelerate transitions

A fourth framework describes a closely related mechanism:

- Adiabatic theorem: the rate of environmental change — which can be understood as the rate at which the system's interaction context shifts — determines smooth versus discontinuous traversal. (Note: the adiabatic theorem is strictly about rate of Hamiltonian change, not measurement-type interaction. Its connection to the interaction picture is through the fact that the Hamiltonian reflects the system's environmental coupling.)

We coin and introduce for the first time:

> **The Navigation Principle.** Interaction is the steering mechanism of state-space traversal. What a system interacts with, how frequently, and at what rate determines its trajectory through state space — either maintaining its current course (Zeno, adiabatic slow regime) or transitioning to new regions (anti-Zeno, adiabatic fast regime). Traversal is constrained by conservation laws (Noether) and geometric structure (symplectic), shaped by the stationary action principle, and realised as the interference of all possible paths (Feynman). Information is preserved throughout (unitarity). Coined and introduced here for the first time. Does not exist in prior literature. The individual frameworks are established science. The convergence observation, the derived principle, and the coinage are original contributions of this paper.

This extends The Deeper Ground's Level 4 finding (*Relational Actualisation* — definiteness is produced through interaction). Level 4 said interaction produces definiteness. The Navigation Principle extends: **interaction doesn't just produce definiteness — it determines the trajectory.**

## 3. The Optimisation Principle

### 3.1 The Question

Given that interaction steers traversal (Navigation Principle), and given preferences over configurations, what does established mathematics and physics say about optimising the trajectory?

Preferences are taken as given — they are the agent's input, not derived from physics. The question is: given preferences, what does mathematics say about optimal traversal?

### 3.2 Eight Independent Frameworks

**Framework 10: Calculus of Variations (Euler, 1744; Lagrange, 1788).** The mathematics of finding paths that optimise a given functional. The Euler-Lagrange equation determines the optimal path for any well-defined quantity to be extremised. Foundation of optimisation theory.

*Direct statement:* For any well-defined quantity to optimise over a trajectory, optimal paths exist and satisfy the Euler-Lagrange equation.

**Framework 11: Pontryagin's Maximum Principle (Pontryagin et al., 1962).** Published in *The Mathematical Theory of Optimal Processes*. Given a dynamical system, available controls, and an objective function, the principle determines the optimal control strategy.

*Direct statement:* For a system with available control inputs, the trajectory optimising a given objective satisfies specific mathematical conditions. Optimal strategy is characterised.

**Framework 12: Bellman's Principle of Optimality (Bellman, 1957).** Published in *Dynamic Programming* (Princeton University Press). An optimal policy has the property that the remaining decisions must constitute an optimal policy from the resulting state.

*Direct statement:* Optimal traversal decomposes into sequential locally optimal decisions. The entire infinite trajectory need not be planned. The best next step from the current position suffices.

**Framework 13: Gradient Flows (Cauchy, 1847; Riemannian formulation).** On a space with a metric, the direction of fastest increase of a function is its gradient. The locally optimal move — the step that most increases any given function — is the gradient step.

*Direct statement:* The direction of fastest improvement is the gradient direction of the function being optimised.

**Framework 14: Lyapunov Stability (Lyapunov, 1892).** Published in *The General Problem of the Stability of Motion*. If a scalar function decreases monotonically along a trajectory, the system is guaranteed to converge to the region where it is minimised. Conversely for increasing functions.

*Direct statement:* If each step increases a preference function, convergence to the preferred region is mathematically guaranteed. Not probable — guaranteed. The guarantee is conditional: Lyapunov proves that monotonic increase implies convergence. It does not prove that monotonic increase is achievable in all state spaces. The condition must be met; the theorem guarantees the consequence.

**Framework 15: Dissipative Structures (Prigogine, 1977).** Nobel Prize 1977. Systems far from equilibrium can spontaneously develop and maintain ordered structures through sustained environmental interaction.

*Direct statement:* Sustained directed interaction can create and stabilise ordered configurations — not just pass through them but maintain them.

**Framework 16: The Fluctuation-Dissipation Theorem (Callen & Welton, 1951; Kubo, 1966).** Published in *Physical Review* and *Reports on Progress in Physics*. A system's natural fluctuations determine which directions of traversal are easiest.

*Direct statement:* Navigation is easier along directions of natural variability.

*Caveat:* Strictly applies near equilibrium. Far-from-equilibrium systems may deviate.

**Framework 17: Geodesics (Riemann, 1854; Einstein, 1915).** On any space with geometric structure, geodesics are the extremal paths — the naturally efficient trajectories determined by the space's curvature.

*Direct statement:* The geometry of the space itself determines naturally efficient paths. An optimising traversal follows the space's own preferred routes.

*Caveat:* Requires the space to have a well-defined metric. The Deeper Ground established abstract geometric structure (Level 9) but a global Riemannian metric on configuration space is an assumption.

### 3.3 Convergence

Eight frameworks. All established mathematics and physics.

They converge on a unified optimisation picture:

We coin and introduce for the first time:

> **The Optimisation Principle.** Given preferences over configurations, optimal traversal is achievable and is characterised by: (1) optimal paths exist for any well-defined preference function (calculus of variations); (2) the optimal interaction strategy at each step is mathematically determined (Pontryagin); (3) optimal traversal decomposes into sequential locally optimal decisions — the best next step from the current position suffices (Bellman); (4) the locally optimal direction is the gradient of the preference function (gradient flows); (5) if each step increases preference, convergence to preferred regions is mathematically guaranteed (Lyapunov); (6) sustained interaction can stabilise preferred configurations once reached (Prigogine); (7) the space itself has naturally efficient paths and directions of easier traversal (geodesics, fluctuation-dissipation). Coined and introduced here for the first time. Does not exist in prior literature. The individual frameworks are established science. The convergence observation, the derived principle, and the coinage are original contributions of this paper.

## 4. The Expansion Principle

### 4.1 The Question

Navigation (Section 2) and optimisation (Section 3) describe a system following a trajectory — one configuration at a time. But must traversal be sequential? What does physics say about simultaneous multi-configuration presence, its growth over time, and its persistence?

### 4.2 Eight Independent Frameworks

**Framework 18: Quantum Superposition (Dirac, 1930; von Neumann, 1932).** Textbook quantum mechanics. A quantum state can be a superposition of multiple eigenstates simultaneously. The system is genuinely in multiple states at once — not in one state with unknown identity (Bell's theorem and Kochen-Specker rule out hidden-variable explanations for all quantum systems).

*Direct statement:* Simultaneous multi-configuration occupation is the quantum default.

**Framework 19: Entanglement Growth and Scrambling (Hayden & Preskill, 2007; Sekino & Susskind, 2008).** Published in *Journal of High Energy Physics*. Quantum information spreads across degrees of freedom through interaction. In strongly interacting systems, the spreading is exponential. The number of entangled degrees of freedom — and therefore the effective dimensionality of the system's quantum state — grows through interaction.

*Direct statement:* The number of entangled degrees of freedom grows through interaction, exponentially in strongly interacting systems. The system's effective presence across state space expands.

**Framework 20: Everettian Branching (Everett, 1957; Wallace, 2012).** Under Everettian QM, each quantum interaction produces branching. Branches grow monotonically. Decoherence ensures branches don't recombine.

*Direct statement:* The number of simultaneously realised configurations grows monotonically and irreversibly.

*Caveat:* This is the Everettian interpretation. Other interpretations don't describe branching this way. Everett is one of our Level 1 frameworks and is mainstream physics.

**Framework 21: Unitarity — Persistence (Schrödinger, 1926; information conservation).** Unitary evolution preserves all information. No quantum state is lost. Every configuration the system has occupied remains encoded in the full state.

*Direct statement:* Every accessed configuration persists. Nothing is lost.

**Framework 22: The Holographic Bound (Bekenstein, 1973; Bousso, 2002).** The maximum number of distinguishable states in a region scales with boundary area. This establishes that the information capacity of a region grows with its boundary.

*Direct statement:* The information capacity — and therefore the maximum number of distinguishable configurations — of a region scales with its boundary area. *Structural implication:* as a system's effective boundary grows through interaction with more of its environment, the capacity for distinguishable configurations increases.

**Framework 23: Quantum Darwinism (Zurek, 2009).** Published in *Nature Physics*. Certain states become redundantly encoded across environmental fragments, achieving robust persistence.

*Direct statement:* Configurations achieve persistence proportional to the breadth of interaction that encodes them.

**Framework 24: Tensor Product Spaces (von Neumann, 1932; Nielsen & Chuang, 2000).** The Hilbert space of a composite system is the tensor product of its components. Each new interaction partner exponentially expands the dimensionality of the effectively occupied space.

*Direct statement:* The dimensionality of simultaneously accessible configuration space grows exponentially with each new interaction.

**Framework 25: Liouville's Theorem (Liouville, 1838).** Phase-space volume is preserved under Hamiltonian evolution. A distribution cannot shrink in total volume — but it can change shape, stretching into thin filaments that spread across ever-larger regions of phase space while preserving total volume. At the coarse-grained level — the level at which macroscopic systems operate — this filamentation constitutes effective expansion: the system's presence spreads across more regions even though fine-grained volume is conserved. This coarse-grained spreading is precisely what entropy increase describes.

*Direct statement:* Fine-grained phase-space volume is preserved. Coarse-grained presence across state space can effectively expand but cannot contract. The system spreads across more of the space over time.

### 4.3 Convergence

Eight frameworks. All established physics and mathematics.

We coin and introduce for the first time:

> **The Expansion Principle.** Simultaneous multi-configuration presence is the quantum default (superposition). Through interaction, this presence expands — exponentially in dimensionality (tensor products), monotonically in realised branches (Everettian QM), and with exponential information spreading (scrambling). This expansion is irreversible: unitarity forbids information loss, Liouville forbids contraction of coarse-grained phase-space presence, and decoherence-driven branching does not reverse. Configurations once accessed persist (unitarity, quantum Darwinism), with persistence proportional to the breadth of their environmental encoding. Coined and introduced here for the first time. Does not exist in prior literature. The individual frameworks are established science. The convergence observation, the derived principle, and the coinage are original contributions of this paper.

## 5. The Unified Principle

### 5.1 Combination

The three principles were independently derived from different framework sets. Their combination is deductive — a logical consequence of the three findings taken together.

The Navigation Principle says: interaction steers traversal.
The Optimisation Principle says: interaction can be directed by preferences, with convergence to preferred configurations guaranteed.
The Expansion Principle says: interaction produces expanding, cumulative, irreversible multi-configuration presence.

A single interaction event does all three simultaneously — it steers (Navigation), it can be preference-directed (Optimisation), and it expands presence (Expansion). These are not three separate activities. They are three aspects of one process.

### 5.2 The Combined Dynamics

If each interaction is preference-directed (Optimisation), and each interaction expands the system's configuration-space presence (Expansion), and the expansion is irreversible (Expansion), then:

Preference-directed interaction produces an irreversibly expanding presence across preferred regions of configuration space.

The expansion is cumulative — each interaction adds configurations. The directionality is maintained — each interaction increases preference. The persistence is guaranteed — unitarity and Liouville forbid contraction. Sustained interaction stabilises reached configurations (Prigogine). And expanded presence creates more interaction possibilities (holographic bound, tensor products), which produce further expansion — a self-reinforcing dynamic.

### 5.3 The Unified Principle

We coin and introduce for the first time:

> **The Unified Principle.** Preference-directed interaction produces an irreversibly expanding, cumulative, stable, self-reinforcing presence across preferred configurations of state space. The process cannot reverse — expansion is guaranteed by unitarity and Liouville (at the coarse-grained level), convergence to preferred regions is guaranteed by Lyapunov (conditional on preference-increasing interactions), and stabilisation is guaranteed by Prigogine. The Unified Principle is the logical combination of the Navigation, Optimisation, and Expansion Principles. No additional frameworks are required — the combination is deductive. Coined and introduced here for the first time. Does not exist in prior literature.

## 6. The Agent Derivation

### 6.1 The Question

Do the Navigation, Optimisation, Expansion, and Unified Principles apply to conscious agents such as human beings? This section derives the answer from convergence rather than assuming it.

Preferences are the agent's input — they are not derived from physics. What is derived is that the *mechanisms* described by the four principles apply to agents as physical systems.

### 6.2 Five Frameworks

**Framework 26: The Universality of Quantum Mechanics (Everett, 1957; Zeh, 1970; Zurek, 2003).** Quantum mechanics applies to all physical systems without exception. There is no quantum/classical boundary. Decoherence explains why macroscopic systems appear classical. The underlying dynamics are quantum. This is the standard understanding within decoherence theory.

*Direct statement:* Human beings are physical systems. The physics of state-space traversal applies to all physical systems. Therefore it applies to us.

**Framework 27: The Perspectival Principle (Mala, 2026l).** Derived in *The Deeper Ground* from five established physics frameworks. We are configurations within the infinite configuration space. Our experience is what the totality looks like from our relational vantage point.

*Direct statement:* We are configurations within the configuration space that the Navigation, Optimisation, and Expansion Principles describe. (This is a prior derivation from this research programme, not an assumption.)

**Framework 28: Neural Plasticity (Hebb, 1949; Kandel, 2000).** Hebb's rule (*The Organization of Behavior*, 1949) established that neural pathways are strengthened by use. Kandel (Nobel Prize 2000) provided the molecular basis. Our neural substrate is physically modified by interaction — new interactions create new connections, repeated interactions strengthen existing ones.

*Direct statement:* Our physical substrate is literally modified by interaction. This is the Navigation Principle instantiated in our neurology.

*Caveat:* Neuroscience, not physics. However, neural plasticity is a physical process governed by molecular and cellular physics.

**Framework 29: Thermodynamics of Living Systems (Schrödinger, 1944; England, 2013).** Schrödinger's *What Is Life?* established that living systems are far-from-equilibrium dissipative structures maintained by environmental interaction. England (2013, *Journal of Chemical Physics*) formalised this further.

*Direct statement:* We are dissipative structures — systems that maintain ordered configurations through sustained environmental interaction. We are precisely the kind of system Prigogine's framework (Framework 15) describes.

**Framework 30: Integrated Information Theory (Tononi, 2004, 2008; Oizumi, Albantakis & Tononi, 2014).** Published in *BMC Neuroscience*, *Biological Bulletin*, and *PLOS Computational Biology*. IIT proposes that consciousness is identical to integrated information (Φ). Experience IS physical configuration. Preference and choice ARE physical processes within configuration space.

*Direct statement:* If IIT is correct, our subjective experience of navigating, choosing, and preferring IS the physics described by the four principles, experienced from within.

*Caveat:* IIT is the most mathematically formalised theory of consciousness available but it is contested, not consensus. Without IIT, the agent derivation still holds through Frameworks 26-29 — we are physical systems and the physics applies. IIT adds the identification of subjective experience with physical configuration.

### 6.3 Convergence

Five frameworks converge:

- Universality of QM: we are physical systems; the physics applies
- Perspectival Principle: we are configurations within the configuration space
- Neural plasticity: our substrate is modified by interaction
- Thermodynamics: we are dissipative structures maintained by interaction
- IIT (flagged as contested): our experience IS physical configuration

The derivation:

> **The Agent Derivation.** The Navigation, Optimisation, Expansion, and Unified Principles apply to conscious agents because: (1) conscious agents are physical systems and quantum mechanics is universal (Framework 26); (2) conscious agents are configurations within the configuration space these principles describe (Framework 27); (3) the physical substrate of conscious agents is modified by interaction, instantiating the Navigation Principle neurologically (Framework 28); (4) conscious agents are dissipative structures maintained by sustained interaction, instantiating the Optimisation Principle thermodynamically (Framework 29); and (5) if IIT is accepted, the subjective experience of navigation, choice, and preference is itself physical configuration within the state space (Framework 30, flagged as contested). The Agent Derivation is an original contribution of this paper, derived from convergence rather than assumed.

## 7. Implications

This section describes what the combined findings imply for agents within the configuration space. It is descriptive, not prescriptive. It describes the mechanism — what physics says about how traversal works for preference-bearing physical systems. It does not recommend any particular course of action.

### 7.1 Interaction Is the Mechanism

The Navigation Principle identifies interaction as the steering mechanism. Combined with the Agent Derivation: what an agent interacts with determines their trajectory through configuration space. This is not metaphor. It is the physics of state-space traversal applied to physical systems that we are.

What an agent attends to, engages with, relates to — these are interactions. Each interaction actualises the next configuration (Relational Actualisation, The Deeper Ground Level 4) and steers the trajectory. The agent's trajectory is constituted by their interactions.

### 7.2 Local Optimisation Is Sufficient

Bellman's principle (Framework 12) directly states that optimal traversal decomposes into sequential locally optimal decisions. An agent does not need to see the entire infinite map. They need to make the best next interaction from their current configuration. Each locally optimal step builds toward a globally optimal trajectory.

Combined with gradient flows (Framework 13): the locally optimal interaction is the one that most increases the preference function from the current position. The direction of improvement is determinable locally.

### 7.3 Convergence Is Guaranteed

Lyapunov stability (Framework 14) directly states that if each interaction increases preference, convergence to preferred regions is mathematically guaranteed. Not probable. Guaranteed. The condition is consistency — each interaction must increase preference.

### 7.4 Expansion Is Natural and Irreversible

The Expansion Principle states that interaction produces cumulative, expanding presence across configuration space. For an agent: interacting expands the range of configurations they are present in. This expansion is irreversible — contraction is mathematically forbidden (Liouville, unitarity). An agent's configuration-space presence can only grow.

### 7.5 The Combined Picture

For any agent that directs their interactions toward preferred configurations: the trajectory converges toward preferred regions (Lyapunov). The presence across preferred regions expands (Expansion Principle). The expansion is irreversible (unitarity, Liouville). The expansion is self-reinforcing — more presence creates more interaction possibilities creates more expansion (holographic bound, tensor products). Preferred configurations can be stabilised through sustained interaction (Prigogine).

This is what the physics implies. Whether any agent chooses to direct their interactions this way is entirely their own affair.

## 8. Infinitography: The Field That Emerges

### 8.1 The Territory

The companion papers mapped the infinite configuration space — its structure, geometry, properties, and constraints. This paper derived the principles of traversal, optimisation, and expansion within that space. Together they define a territory and the principles of operating within it.

The study of this territory — the infinite configuration space of reality, its structure, and how systems operate within it — is a field. It is analogous to geography, which studies the Earth's surface, and cartography, which maps it. But the territory here is not the surface of a planet. It is the infinite space of all possible configurations of reality.

Just as the Age of Exploration was enabled by the combination of maps (cartography), protocols (navigation), and practice (seamanship and exploration), the study of the infinite configuration space is enabled by the combination of structural findings (the companion papers), traversal principles (this paper), and the practice of applying them.

### 8.2 Definition

We coin and introduce for the first time:

> **Infinitography** is the study, exploration, and navigation of the infinite configuration space of reality. It encompasses the structure of the space, the principles of traversal within it, the dynamics of expansion across it, and the methods of exploring its unmapped regions. Infinitography is to the infinite configuration space what geography and navigation are to the Earth's surface — the field that studies the territory and develops the principles of operating within it. Coined and introduced here for the first time. Does not exist in prior literature.

### 8.3 Subfields

Six subfields emerge naturally from the findings. All are coined and introduced here for the first time. None exist in prior literature.

**Configuration Space Cartography.** The study and mapping of the infinite configuration space itself — its geometry, topology, dimensionality, symmetries, conservation laws, constraints, and the structure of the infinite possibility space it contains. This includes mapping the possibility space itself, which may constitute an infinite field of study in its own right. The companion papers (Infinite Ground, Deeper Ground) are the first contributions. Future work maps more of the space's properties and the structure of possibility itself.

**Traversal Dynamics.** The study of how systems move through the configuration space — the mechanisms of navigation. The Navigation Principle (Section 2) is the first result. This subfield encompasses the physics of state-space traversal and the development of further traversal protocols.

**Trajectory Optimisation.** The study of how trajectories through configuration space can be optimised given preference functions. The Optimisation Principle (Section 3) is the first result. This subfield encompasses the mathematics of optimal path-finding and the development of further optimisation methods.

**Expansion Dynamics.** The study of how presence across configuration space grows, persists, and self-reinforces. The Expansion Principle (Section 4) is the first result. This subfield encompasses the physics of multi-configuration presence and cumulative irreversible growth.

**Agent Dynamics.** The study of how preference-bearing systems — agents — operate within configuration space. The Agent Derivation (Section 6) is the first result. This subfield encompasses the application of traversal, optimisation, and expansion principles to conscious agents.

**Exploration Theory.** The study of how unmapped regions of configuration space are discovered and accessed. This paper does not derive a specific exploration protocol — but the field is defined by the existence of the space (Level 1 of the companion papers), its inexhaustibility (Level 8), and the expansion dynamics (Section 4) that guarantee new regions are continually accessed. Exploration Theory is the open frontier.

## 11. Conclusion

The companion papers asked: what is reality? They derived, across eleven levels and forty-eight frameworks, an answer: reality is an infinite, self-referential, relational, holographic, inexhaustible configuration space.

**Navigation:** Interaction steers. What you interact with determines your trajectory.

**Optimisation:** Preference-directed interaction converges to preferred configurations. The best next step suffices. Convergence is guaranteed.

**Unified:** Preference-directed interaction produces irreversibly expanding, stable, self-reinforcing presence across preferred configurations.

The paper is descriptive. It derives mechanisms. It does not prescribe their use. The protocols derived here are the first within their subfields — and, by the findings of the companion papers (*The Consistency Horizon*, The Deeper Ground Level 11), necessarily among many valid approaches that are structurally guaranteed to exist. The Navigation, Optimisation, and Expansion Principles are one set of derived protocols. The subfields of Infinitography exist precisely to discover the others. The territory is inexhaustible. The exploration has begun.

The companion papers mapped the territory. This paper derived the first principles of operating within it. What comes next belongs to whoever picks up the map.
